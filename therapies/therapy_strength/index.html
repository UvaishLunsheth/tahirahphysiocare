What is Strength Training Therapy?

Strength training therapy focuses on improving muscular strength, joint stability, endurance and functional performance. It is a key part of physiotherapy rehabilitation, especially after injuries, surgery or long periods of muscle inactivity.

Benefits of Physiotherapy-Guided Strength Training

  • Improves muscle power and endurance
  • Enhances joint stability and balance
  • Prevents reinjury and improves movement control
  • Boosts metabolism and functional mobility
  • Reduces chronic pain and muscular stiffness
  • Supports recovery after fractures or surgeries
  • Improves posture and daily activity performance

Who Needs Strength Training Therapy?

  • Patients recovering from fractures or immobilization
  • Post-operative patients (knee replacement, ACL, spine surgery)
  • Individuals with muscle weakness or imbalance
  • People with back, knee, hip or shoulder pain
  • Sports persons recovering from injuries
  • Patients with neurological conditions requiring strengthening

How We Conduct Strength Training at Our Clinic

Your physiotherapist conducts a full strength assessment, identifies weak muscle groups, and develops a progressive training plan. Training typically includes:

  • Resistance band exercises
  • Body-weight strengthening
  • Isometric muscle activation
  • Posture & core stabilization
  • Balance & proprioceptive strengthening
  • Functional movement training
